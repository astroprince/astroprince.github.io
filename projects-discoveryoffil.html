Discovery Of A Filamentary Synchrotron Structure Connected To The Coherent Magnetic Field In The Outer Galaxy

[Sep 2021 - Oct 2022]

Using data from the Galactic Arecibo L-band Feed Array Continuum Transit Survey (GALFACTS), we reported the discovery of two previously unidentified, very compressed, thin, and straight polarized filaments approximately centred at Galactic coordinates, (l, b) = (182.5°, -4.0°), which we call G182.5-4.0. We find evidence indicating that the filaments align with the coherent magnetic field of the outer Galaxy and consider various scenarios that could explain the origin of these filaments. We consider this may represent an example of a new class of object that is neither an SNR nor a bow shock, and the aim of this publication is also to further classify G182.5-4.0. The highly compressed nature of these filaments and their alignment with Galactic plane suggests a scenario where this object formed in a magnetic field that was compressed by the expanding Orion-Eridanus superbubble, suggesting that the object is related to this superbubble and implying a distance of 400 pc.
